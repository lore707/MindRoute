Build a web app called MindRoute with this flow:
PAGE 1 — LANDING
Title: MindRoute
Subtitle: "We don't ask where you want to go. We ask who you are."
Button: "Let's begin"
Design: minimalist, warm background (#FDFAF7), text color #1A1A2E, accent color #E94560, font Georgia for text, Arial for labels and buttons. Smooth transitions between pages.
PAGE 2 — PROFILING
Show an intro message:
"Answer as if no one will judge your choices. You're not planning a trip: you're telling us how you move through the world."
Then show 7 questions ONE AT A TIME:

"Your last trip — did it disappoint or surprise you? What didn't you expect?"
"If you could disappear for a week without telling anyone — and without being able to post anything — where would you go and what would you do?"
"What made you feel most alive in the last year — even if it has nothing to do with travel?"
"When you travel, what puts you more at ease: knowing everything in advance or discovering as you go?"
"Is there a place everyone loves that just doesn't speak to you — not because it's bad, it's just not yours?"
"If this trip were meant to do something for you — without knowing what in advance — what do you hope will be different when you come back?"
"Right now, do you feel more energized or more tired?"

After each answer, show a 1-line micro-reaction ("Got it.", "Interesting.", "Understood.") then move to the next question. Show a progress bar at top: "3 of 7"
After the 7 questions, show a quick form (all visible at once):

How many days do you have? [number field]
When would you leave? [text field]
How much do you want to spend, roughly? [text field]
Where are you departing from? [text field]
Button: "Find me a place"

PAGE 3 — DESTINATIONS
Show 3 destination cards side by side (stacked on mobile). Each card has: place name, "Why this place is yours" text, experience preview, practical info. Below the cards: "Which of these three made you feel something?"
Clicking a card = choosing it. No confirm button.
PAGE 4 — ITINERARY
Vertical flow, day by day. Each day is an expandable section with an evocative title visible and details hidden until clicked. At the bottom: budget summary, packing list, personal closing message. Two buttons: "Save as PDF" and "Start over".
BACKEND LOGIC:
For now, use placeholder/mock data for the AI responses. Create 3 hardcoded destination objects and 1 hardcoded itinerary. I will add the real API calls later. Structure the code so it's easy to replace the mock data with actual API calls to an LLM.
TECH: Use Node.js with Express for backend, vanilla HTML/CSS/JS for frontend. Keep it simple, no unnecessary frameworks.
